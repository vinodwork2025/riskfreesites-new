Five things a website does that social media cannot

1

Shows up on Google when people search for what you do

When someone searches "electrician in Phoenix" or "best coffee shop near me," Google shows websites. Not Instagram pages. Not Facebook profiles. A website puts you in that search result. Without one, you are invisible to people actively looking for your service.

2

Gives customers all the information they need before they call

Your services, prices, location, hours, and who you are. A website answers the questions every new customer has. When they find the answers easily, they are more likely to reach out. When they cannot find them, they find someone else.

3

Captures leads even when you are not working

A contact form on your website takes enquiries at 10pm on a Saturday. You wake up Monday and there is a message waiting. You cannot do that with a phone number alone, and DMs on Instagram are not a reliable lead system.

4

Builds trust before the first conversation

People check businesses online before they contact them. A clean, lead-generation website signals that your business is real and established. No website often means no call, especially for higher-value services.

5

You own it. Nobody can take it away.

Instagram can change its algorithm. They can restrict your account. The platform can become less popular. Your website belongs to you. Nobody can cut your reach or change the rules on you overnight.

Common questions

Can I use just a Google Business Profile instead of a website?
A Google Business Profile helps people find your location and hours. But it cannot explain your services in detail, let people submit a contact form, or rank for service-specific searches. A website does all of that. Both together is better than either alone.
What if most of my customers come from referrals?
Referrals are great. But even referral clients check your website before they call. And a website opens you up to entirely new customers who do not know anyone who has used you. It extends your reach beyond your personal network.
How do I know if I need a website right now?
If competitors with worse services are getting more customers than you, if people cannot easily find you on Google, or if you have no way to take enquiries outside of business hours, you need a website.
