Finding a therapist is a personal decision. Clients want to feel comfortable before they even make the first appointment. They search online. They read about different therapists. They look at photos, bios, and specialties. They pick someone who feels like the right fit.

If you are not showing up in that search, or your website does not tell enough of your story, those clients book with someone else.

What your therapist website will include

Your bio and approach

Your photo, background, training, and therapy style. Clients choose therapists partly on gut feeling. Your bio helps with that.

Specialties and issues you treat

Anxiety, depression, relationships, trauma, grief. Clear descriptions that match what people are searching for.

New client contact form

A form that lets potential clients reach out with their needs. Easier than calling for many people who are nervous about starting therapy.

Insurance and fees

Whether you accept insurance, your session rates, and sliding scale options if applicable. Clients want this upfront.

Location and telehealth info

Your office address, the states you are licensed in, and whether you offer online sessions.

Local SEO

Set up to appear when people search for therapists in your city or state. More visibility without paid ads.
