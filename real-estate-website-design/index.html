The Problem

Your brokerage page is not your website.

Most agents have a profile page on their brokerage site. It has your photo, your phone number, and a list of active listings. That is not enough. When a buyer or seller searches for an agent online, they want to know who you are, what areas you specialize in, and why they should pick you over the other agents in town.

A personal website gives you that space. It is your name, your brand, your story. You control what it says, what it shows, and how it ranks in local searches. A brokerage page cannot do that. It ranks for the brokerage, not for you.

Agents with personal websites get found on Google by name, by area, and by specialty. They look more professional than agents without one. And they capture contact details from visitors who are not yet ready to call but want to stay in touch.

Questions

Things agents ask us

Can I add my own listings to the website?
Yes. We can set up a listings section where you add, update, and remove properties yourself. No technical skills needed. If you want featured properties highlighted on the homepage, we can do that too.
Will buyers be able to search listings on my site?
For a basic demo, we showcase your featured listings. Full MLS integration is a bigger project that we handle after you approve the site. Start with seeing what your brand and website look like first.
How does the website help me get leads?
Your site will rank on Google for local agent searches over time. It also gives you a real page to share in social posts, ads, and email signatures. Every visitor sees a contact form they can fill in to get in touch or request a property valuation.
I already have a page on my brokerage site. Do I still need this?
Your brokerage page helps them find the brokerage. Your personal site helps people find you. If you ever change firms, your personal website stays with you. Your leads, your brand, your name. That is yours to keep.
